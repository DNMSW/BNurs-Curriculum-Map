# NURS31361: Nurse as a Leader, Manager and Coordinator of Care
## Curriculum Coverage Gap Analysis & Remediation Plan

**Document Date:** 18 May 2026  
**Unit Code:** NURS31361  
**Level:** 6 (Master's level - Year 3)  
**Credits:** 20  
**Fields:** Adult, Mental Health, Children & Young People

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Analysis of the NURS31361 unit descriptor against current curriculum mapping has identified **5 critical gaps** representing significant deficiencies in programme-level learning outcomes. These gaps span essential leadership competencies that should be mandatory for graduate nurses.

**Risk Level:** 🔴 **HIGH** – Multiple outcomes lack explicit teaching, assessment, or integration across the programme.

---

## PRIORITY 1: CRITICAL GAPS (Currently Unmapped/Missing)

### Gap 1.1: Health Economics & Resource Allocation
**NURS31361 Outcome:** A5 - *Understand the principles and application of health economics and their relevance to resource allocation in health and social care organisations*

**NMC Standards Addressed:** 7.3, 7.12  
**Programme Outcomes Addressed:** A5, A8 (potentially)

| Element | Status | Finding |
|---------|--------|---------|
| **Current Coverage** | ❌ MISSING | No mapped unit outcomes explicitly teach health economics or resource allocation |
| **Implicit Coverage** | ⚠️ WEAK | NURS31342 touches on policy/social context but lacks economics focus |
| **Assessment** | ❌ NONE | No summative assessment identified for this outcome |

**Why This Matters:**
- NMC proficiency 7.3 explicitly requires understanding health economics principles
- NMC 7.12 requires ability to develop business cases for care funding
- Graduates must make resource-informed clinical decisions
- Essential for role-readiness as coordinators of care

**Recommended Integration Points (Priority Order):**

1. **PRIMARY: NURS31361 (Nurse as a Leader, Manager and Coordinator of Care)**
   - **New Learning Outcome (Knowledge & Understanding):**  
     *"Understand the principles of health economics, cost-effectiveness analysis, and resource allocation models; apply these to justify care decisions and develop simple business cases for service improvements"*
   - **Assessment:** Essay or case study analysis demonstrating application to care coordination scenarios
   - **NMC Mapping:** 7.3, 7.12
   - **Why Here:** Directly aligns with unit purpose; students learn management and coordination at this level

2. **SECONDARY: NURS31342 (Advancing Care - Social & Political Context)**
   - **Enhanced Existing Outcome:**  
     Current: *"Conduct policy analysis..."*  
     **Add:** *"...including analysis of health economic implications, cost-benefit considerations, and resource constraints on care delivery"*
   - **Assessment:** Extend existing essay to include economics analysis
   - **NMC Mapping:** 7.2, 7.3, 7.4

3. **TERTIARY: Year 3 Clinical Placements (NURS31311/312/322)**
   - **New Practical Outcome:**  
     *"Identify and discuss real-world examples of resource allocation decisions observed in placement; reflect on how economics influenced care decisions"*
   - **Assessment:** PARE portfolio addition
   - **Why Here:** Contextualise learning in authentic practice environments

### Gap 1.2: Active Listening & Interpersonal Interaction Skills (Advanced Level)
**NURS31361 Outcome:** A2 - *Demonstrate effective person and team management by active listening, and selecting appropriate strategies for positive outcomes of interaction*

**NMC Standards Addressed:** 1.5, 4.2, 5.6, 5.9  
**Programme Outcomes Addressed:** A2, B5, D2, D4

| Element | Status | Finding |
|---------|--------|---------|
| **Current Coverage** | ⚠️ PARTIAL | Taught in foundational units (NURS10091, 10052, 10061) |
| **Advanced Application** | ❌ MISSING | No explicit Level 6 teaching of active listening in management/team contexts |
| **Assessment** | ⚠️ WEAK | Fragmented across multiple units; no coherent progression to advanced level |

**Why This Matters:**
- Critical for leadership and managing difficult conversations
- Students reach Level 6 without explicit advanced coaching in listening during conflict, appraisal, or feedback scenarios
- NMC 5.6 requires exhibiting leadership through guidance and support
- Currently students rely on implicit learning rather than structured development

**Recommended Integration Points (Priority Order):**

1. **PRIMARY: NURS31361 (Nurse as a Leader, Manager and Coordinator of Care)**
   - **New Learning Outcome (Intellectual Skills):**  
     *"Demonstrate advanced active listening and communication techniques in complex team interactions; select and justify use of communication strategies for managing challenging conversations, feedback delivery, and conflict resolution"*
   - **Assessment:** Recorded roleplay with feedback analysis OR case-based written reflection on communication strategy selection
   - **NMC Mapping:** 1.5, 4.2, 5.6, 5.9
   - **Why Here:** Directly supports leadership and team management outcomes; Level 6 application

2. **SECONDARY: NURS31301 (Nurse as Facilitator of Learning)**
   - **Enhanced Existing Outcome:**  
     Current: *"Enhanced IT skills and use of other technologies. Selection of appropriate communication strategies..."*  
     **Add explicit component:** *"...including active listening techniques, non-verbal communication awareness, and adapting communication for diverse learners"*
   - **Assessment:** Teaching session evaluation includes peer feedback on communication effectiveness
   - **NMC Mapping:** 1.5, 5.8, 5.10

3. **TERTIARY: Year 3 Clinical Placements (NURS31311/312/322)**
   - **New Practical Outcome:**  
     *"Demonstrate advanced communication and active listening in team huddles, handovers, and interdisciplinary meetings; reflect on use of specific techniques"*
   - **Assessment:** PARE portfolio with evidence of observer feedback
   - **Why Here:** Practise in authentic leadership contexts; gather 360-degree feedback

### Gap 1.3: Alternative Communication Techniques for Complex Needs
**NURS31361 Outcome:** A4 - *Consider evidence based best practice approaches for supporting people in families, teams and with understanding of those who require alternative communication techniques*

**NMC Standards Addressed:** 1.8, 1.13, 2.1, 2.5, 5.8, 6.9, 7.13  
**Programme Outcomes Addressed:** C2, D2

| Element | Status | Finding |
|---------|--------|---------|
| **Current Coverage** | ⚠️ SCATTERED | Mentioned in multiple units (NURS10091, 31301) but no systematic framework |
| **Advanced Application** | ❌ MISSING | No Level 6 unit explicitly teaches or assesses use of AAC, makaton, visual supports, etc. in leadership contexts |
| **Assessment** | ❌ NONE | No mapped summative assessment for this outcome at Level 6 |

**Why This Matters:**
- Nursing care involves diverse communication needs (learning disability, autism, aphasia, dementia, etc.)
- At Level 6, students should be able to support teams in implementing alternative approaches
- NMC 1.8 requires critical thinking about communication for diverse populations
- Leadership requires advocating for and implementing inclusive communication

**Recommended Integration Points (Priority Order):**

1. **PRIMARY: NURS31361 (Nurse as a Leader, Manager and Coordinator of Care)**
   - **New Learning Outcome (Practical Skills):**  
     *"Lead and advocate for implementation of appropriate alternative communication techniques for individuals with complex communication needs; support and coach team members in use of AAC strategies, Makaton, visual supports, and other evidence-based approaches"*
   - **Assessment:** Case study or project work: design and justify implementation of communication accessibility approach for specific clinical scenario
   - **NMC Mapping:** 1.8, 1.13, 5.8, 6.9
   - **Why Here:** Leadership focus on advocacy and team support; Level 6 strategic thinking

2. **SECONDARY: NURS31301 (Nurse as Facilitator of Learning)**
   - **Enhanced Existing Outcome:**  
     Add: *"...recognising and accommodating diverse communication needs of learners, including use of visual aids, pacing, and adapted materials"*
   - **Assessment:** Teaching session includes reflection on how communication was adapted
   - **NMC Mapping:** 1.13, 5.8

3. **TERTIARY: Discovery Units or Optional Modules**
   - Consider adding a small specialist module on communication accessibility in complex healthcare settings
   - Could support learning for CYP and MH specialists particularly

### Gap 1.4: Supervision Frameworks & Developmental Appraisal
**NURS31361 Outcome:** A6 - *Demonstrate effective supervision, teaching and performance appraisal through use of clear communication and individual encouragement*

**NMC Standards Addressed:** 4.1, 5.6, 5.7, 5.8, 5.9, 5.10  
**Programme Outcomes Addressed:** A6, B5, B6

| Element | Status | Finding |
|---------|--------|---------|
| **Current Coverage** | ⚠️ PARTIAL | NURS31301 covers teaching and facilitation; Year 3 placements include some supervision exposure |
| **Appraisal & Performance Management** | ❌ MISSING | No explicit unit teaches performance appraisal frameworks, developmental conversations, or linking to NDR/NMC requirements |
| **Assessment** | ⚠️ WEAK | No summative assessment of appraisal skills; only implicit through placement observations |

**Why This Matters:**
- Registered nurses are increasingly expected to conduct appraisals and supervision
- NMC Standard 5.7 explicitly requires ability to monitor and evaluate quality of care delivered by others
- NMC 5.8 & 5.10 require supervision and feedback capabilities
- Currently students graduate without structured learning in this critical management skill

**Recommended Integration Points (Priority Order):**

1. **PRIMARY: NURS31361 (Nurse as a Leader, Manager and Coordinator of Care)**
   - **New Learning Outcome (Practical Skills):**  
     *"Conduct or simulate effective supervision meetings; conduct or participate in performance appraisal processes; demonstrate ability to give constructive developmental feedback using evidence-based frameworks (e.g., 1:1 mentoring, formal appraisal structures)"*
   - **Assessment:** Recorded supervision/appraisal roleplay with rubric covering: psychological safety, developmental focus, clarity of feedback, action planning
   - **NMC Mapping:** 4.1, 5.6, 5.7, 5.8, 5.9, 5.10
   - **Why Here:** Core management competency; Level 6 application; integrates with care coordination role

2. **SECONDARY: NURS31380 (BNurs Year 3 Professional Development)**
   - **New or Enhanced Outcome:**  
     *"Demonstrate understanding of supervision frameworks, appraisal processes, and professional development planning relevant to registered nurse role"*
   - **Assessment:** Portfolio entry or reflective assignment
   - **Why Here:** Professional development focus; supports transition to registered practice

3. **TERTIARY: Year 3 Clinical Placements (NURS31311/312/322)**
   - **New Practical Outcome:**  
     *"Observe formal supervision or appraisal processes; reflect on effectiveness; practice giving and receiving feedback in team contexts"*
   - **Assessment:** PARE portfolio with reflection
   - **Why Here:** Observe authentic processes; develop comfort with feedback culture

### Gap 1.5: Person-Centred Advocacy & Reasonable Adjustments (Advanced Application)
**NURS31361 Outcome:** A7 - *Provide and promote non-discriminatory person centred and sensitive understanding at all times*

**NMC Standards Addressed:** 1.4, 1.9, 1.12, 1.14, 7.9  
**Programme Outcomes Addressed:** C4, B7, D1

| Element | Status | Finding |
|---------|--------|---------|
| **Current Coverage** | ⚠️ PARTIAL | NURS10091 (Values Based Nursing) and placements cover foundational aspects |
| **Advanced Application** | ❌ MISSING | No Level 6 unit explicitly teaches advocacy in systemic/organisational contexts or leading reasonable adjustments |
| **Assessment** | ⚠️ WEAK | Assessed implicitly through values but no explicit assessment of advocacy action or systemic change |

**Why This Matters:**
- Leadership role requires advocating for vulnerable groups at organisational level
- NMC 7.9 explicitly requires facilitating equitable access for vulnerable/disabled people
- Students should graduate able to challenge systemic discrimination, not just individual prejudice
- Currently lacks explicit framework for advocacy in management contexts

**Recommended Integration Points (Priority Order):**

1. **PRIMARY: NURS31361 (Nurse as a Leader, Manager and Coordinator of Care)**
   - **Enhanced Existing Outcome:**  
     Current: *"Provide person-centred advocacy"*  
     **Strengthen to:** *"Lead implementation of person-centred advocacy strategies; identify and challenge barriers to equitable access; develop and champion reasonable adjustments for individuals with disabilities or complex needs; evaluate effectiveness of advocacy interventions"*
   - **Assessment:** Case study or improvement project: identify systemic barrier to equity, design intervention, evaluate (e.g., accessible communication, reasonable adjustments for disabled staff member, advocacy for unpopular patient choice)
   - **NMC Mapping:** 1.4, 1.9, 1.12, 1.14, 7.9
   - **Why Here:** Strategic advocacy fits leadership/coordination role; systemic level (not just individual)

2. **SECONDARY: NURS31342 (Advancing Care - Social & Political Context)**
   - **Enhanced Existing Outcome:**  
     Current: *"Demonstrate a critical awareness of social exclusion..."*  
     **Add:** *"...and translate critical awareness into advocacy and action within professional practice"*
   - **Assessment:** Essay includes examples of personal advocacy action or reflection on barriers encountered
   - **NMC Mapping:** 1.4, 1.9, 7.2, 7.4

3. **TERTIARY: Year 3 Clinical Placements**
   - **New Practical Outcome:**  
     *"Identify a person-centred or equity issue in placement; advocate appropriately; reflect on barriers and facilitators to advocacy"*
   - **Assessment:** PARE portfolio with evidence of advocacy action

## PRIORITY 2: SIGNIFICANT GAPS (Partially Addressed But Insufficient for Level 6)

### Gap 2.1: Interdisciplinary Team Role Clarity & Coordination
**NURS31361 Outcome:** B1 - *Demonstrate an understanding of the roles, responsibilities and scope of practice of all members of the nursing and interdisciplinary team*

**NMC Standards Addressed:** 1.9, 1.18, 5.2, 5.4, 6.12, 7.1  
**Programme Outcomes Addressed:** A1, D6

| Element | Status | Finding |
|---------|--------|---------|
| **Current Coverage** | ⚠️ MODERATE | Scattered across NURS10052, 10091, 23110, 31311-322 |
| **Advanced Application** | ⚠️ WEAK | No Level 6 unit explicitly teaches MDT role mapping, scope negotiation, or conflict resolution across professions |
| **Assessment** | ⚠️ WEAK | Implicit in placement assessments; no explicit summative assessment |

**Remediation Recommendation:**

**NURS31361 Enhancement:**
- **Add to Intellectual Skills Outcome:**  
  *"...map MDT roles and responsibilities; identify and negotiate overlapping/contested scope; propose appropriate referral/escalation pathways..."*
- **Assessment:** MDT role mapping exercise or case study (e.g., "Design ideal MDT for patient with complex needs; justify roles and communication flow")
- **Why Here:** Direct support for care coordination role

### Gap 2.2: Quality Improvement & Audit Methodology
**NURS31361 Outcome:** B3/C5 - *Demonstrate the ability to monitor and evaluate the quality of care delivered by others; manage risks and take proactive measures to improve quality*

**NMC Standards Addressed:** 5.7, 6.1-6.12  
**Programme Outcomes Addressed:** B3, B5, C5

| Element | Status | Finding |
|---------|--------|---------|
| **Current Coverage** | ⚠️ WEAK | Mentioned in NURS31301, 31361, 31342 but no structured curriculum in QI methodologies |
| **Advanced Application** | ❌ MISSING | No unit teaches Plan-Do-Study-Act (PDSA), root cause analysis, or systematic audit cycles |
| **Assessment** | ❌ NONE | No summative assessment of ability to conduct or lead quality improvement |

**Remediation Recommendation:**

**NURS31361 Enhancement:**
- **New Practical Skills Outcome:**  
  *"Design and lead a small quality improvement project or audit cycle; apply quality improvement methodology (e.g., PDSA, lean principles); analyse results and recommend changes"*
- **Assessment:** QI project portfolio including: audit tool development, data collection, analysis, recommendations, implementation plan
- **NMC Mapping:** 5.7, 6.1-6.12
- **Why Here:** Core management responsibility; integrates with monitoring/evaluation

### Gap 2.3: Change Management & Influence Skills
**NURS31361 Outcome:** B4/C5 - *Understand the mechanisms that can be used to influence organisational change and public policy; take proactive measures for quality improvement*

**NMC Standards Addressed:** 5.12, 6.6, 7.2, 7.13  
**Programme Outcomes Addressed:** B4, D1

| Element | Status | Finding |
|---------|--------|---------|
| **Current Coverage** | ⚠️ WEAK | NURS31342 touches on policy; NURS31361 implies but doesn't explicitly teach change management |
| **Advanced Application** | ❌ MISSING | No unit teaches change models (e.g., Kotter, Lewin), stakeholder engagement, or resistance management |
| **Assessment** | ⚠️ WEAK | Implicit in essays; no explicit assessment of change leadership capability |

**Remediation Recommendation:**

**NURS31361 Enhancement:**
- **New Intellectual Skills Outcome:**  
  *"Apply change management frameworks to propose and justify organisational or practice improvements; identify and address stakeholder concerns; plan strategies to reduce resistance to change"*
- **Assessment:** Change project proposal or case analysis using specific change model (e.g., "Propose introduction of new care pathway: apply Kotter's 8-step model, identify key stakeholders, address likely resistance")
- **NMC Mapping:** 5.12, 7.2, 7.13
- **Why Here:** Explicit application of change theory to practice scenarios
